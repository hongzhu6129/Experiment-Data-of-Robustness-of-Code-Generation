

**Test Cases:**

Here are some test cases, including corner and edge cases, that you can use to verify the correctness of the `AverageCalculator` program:

**Normal Cases:**

1. **Input:** 10, 20, 30   **Expected Output:** 20.0
2. **Input:** 1.5, 2.5, 3.5   **Expected Output:** 2.5
3. **Input:** -5, 0, 5   **Expected Output:** 0.0

**Edge Cases:**

4. **Input:** 0, 0, 0  **Expected Output:** 0.0  (All zeros)
5. **Input:** Integer.MAX_VALUE, Integer.MAX_VALUE, Integer.MAX_VALUE **Expected Output:**  2.147483647E9 (Handling large numbers, potential overflow if using `int` instead of `double`)


**Corner Cases (using `double` helps avoid some traditional integer overflow issues, but we still test for extreme values and potential floating-point representation inaccuracies):**

6. **Input:** Double.MAX_VALUE, Double.MAX_VALUE, Double.MAX_VALUE **Expected Output:** Infinity (Handles Infinity correctly)
7. **Input:** Double.MIN_VALUE, Double.MIN_VALUE, Double.MIN_VALUE  **Expected Output:**  A very small positive number close to zero (Handles very small numbers correctly)
8. **Input:** Double.NaN, 10, 20  **Expected Output:** NaN (Handles NaN correctly - any calculation involving NaN results in NaN)
9. **Input:** 1, Double.POSITIVE_INFINITY, 2  **Expected Output:** Infinity
