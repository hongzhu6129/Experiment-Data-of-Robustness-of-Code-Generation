

**Test Cases:**

Here are some test cases including corner cases:

**1. Provided Example:**

* Latitude 1: 25
* Longitude 1: 35
* Latitude 2: 35.5
* Longitude 2: 25.5
* Expected Output: ~1480.08 km

**2. Same Point:**

* Latitude 1: 45
* Longitude 1: 10
* Latitude 2: 45
* Longitude 2: 10
* Expected Output: 0 km

**3. Antipodes (Opposite sides of the Earth):**

* Latitude 1: 45
* Longitude 1: 10
* Latitude 2: -45
* Longitude 2: 170  (10 + 180 modulo 360 is -170, but longitude should be between -180 and 180)
* Expected Output: ~20015.08 km (approximately Earth's diameter * pi / 2 which is half the circumference of Earth)


**4. North and South Poles:**

* Latitude 1: 90  (North Pole)
* Longitude 1: 0  (Longitude is irrelevant at the poles)
* Latitude 2: -90 (South Pole)
* Longitude 2: 10 (Longitude is irrelevant at the poles)
* Expected Output: ~20015.08 km (approximately half the circumference of Earth)


**5.  Points on the Equator, 90 degrees apart:**

* Latitude 1: 0
* Longitude 1: 0
* Latitude 2: 0
* Longitude 2: 90
* Expected Output: ~10007.54 km  (approximately a quarter of the circumference of Earth)



**6. Points near the International Date Line:**

* Latitude 1: 10
* Longitude 1: 179
* Latitude 2: 10
* Longitude 2: -179
* Expected Output: A small distance (as they are close despite being on opposite sides of the date line).


**7. Invalid Input (Handle these in your program):**

* Latitude outside the range -90 to +90.
* Longitude outside the range -180 to +180.
