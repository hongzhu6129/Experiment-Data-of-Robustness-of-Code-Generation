

**Test Cases:**

**Positive Test Cases:**

* Input: "I would like to read a book."
* Expected Output: "I **would** like to read a book."

* Input: "He would be a great addition to our team."
* Expected Output: "He **would** be a great addition to our team."

* Input: "They would love to go on a vacation."
* Expected Output: "They **would** love to go on a vacation."

**Negative Test Cases:**

* Input: "I should like to read a book."
* Expected Output: "I should like to read a book." (No annotation since "would" is not present)

* Input: "He could be a great addition to our team."
* Expected Output: "He could be a great addition to our team." (No annotation since "would" is not present)

* Input: "They might love to go on a vacation."
* Expected Output: "They might love to go on a vacation." (No annotation since "would" is not present)

**Corner Cases:**

* Input: ""
* Expected Output: "" (Empty string)

* Input: " " (a single space)
* Expected Output: " " (a single space)

* Input: "would"
* Expected Output: "**would**" (The entire string is annotated)
