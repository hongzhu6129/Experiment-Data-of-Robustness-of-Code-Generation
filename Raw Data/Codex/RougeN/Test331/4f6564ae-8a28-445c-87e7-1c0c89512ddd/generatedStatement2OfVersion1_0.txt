

**Test Cases:**

Here are some test cases covering normal scenarios, edge cases, and corner cases:

**1. Normal Cases:**

* **Input:** 10, 20, 30
* **Expected Output:** 20.0

* **Input:** 1.5, 2.5, 3.5
* **Expected Output:** 2.5

**2. Edge Cases:**

* **Input:** 0, 0, 0  (All zeros)
* **Expected Output:** 0.0

* **Input:** -10, 20, 10 (Negative and positive numbers)
* **Expected Output:** 6.666666666666667

**3. Corner Cases (Potential Issues):**

* **Input:** Integer.MAX_VALUE, Integer.MAX_VALUE, Integer.MAX_VALUE (Overflow)
* **Expected Output:** Infinity  (or a very large positive number depending on how Java handles overflow)

* **Input:** Integer.MIN_VALUE, Integer.MIN_VALUE, Integer.MIN_VALUE (Underflow)
* **Expected Output:** -Infinity (or a very large negative number)

* **Input:** Double.MAX_VALUE, Double.MAX_VALUE, Double.MAX_VALUE (Double overflow)
* **Expected Output:** Infinity

* **Input:** Double.NaN, 10, 20 (NaN input)
* **Expected Output:** NaN

* **Input:** 10, Double.POSITIVE_INFINITY, 20 (Infinity input)
* **Expected Output:** Infinity
