

**Test Cases:**

Here are some test cases, including corner cases, to thoroughly test the program:

1. **Provided Example:**
   - Latitude 1: 25
   - Longitude 1: 35
   - Latitude 2: 35.5
   - Longitude 2: 25.5
   - Expected Distance: ~1480.08 km

2. **Same Point:**
   - Latitude 1: 40
   - Longitude 1: -74  (New York)
   - Latitude 2: 40
   - Longitude 2: -74  (New York)
   - Expected Distance: 0 km

3. **North and South Poles:**
   - Latitude 1: 90   (North Pole)
   - Longitude 1: 0
   - Latitude 2: -90  (South Pole)
   - Longitude 2: 0    (Longitude doesn't matter at poles)
   - Expected Distance: ~20015.08 km (approximately Earth's circumference / 2)

4. **Opposite Sides of the Earth (Antipodes):**
   - Latitude 1: 40
   - Longitude 1: -74  (New York)
   - Latitude 2: -40
   - Longitude 2: 106  (Near Perth, Australia)
   - Expected Distance: ~20015.08 km (approximately Earth's circumference / 2)

5. **Points near the Prime Meridian & 180th Meridian:**
   - Latitude 1: 0
   - Longitude 1: 0.1  (Just east of Prime Meridian)
   - Latitude 2: 0
   - Longitude 2: -179.9 (Just west of 180th Meridian)
   - Expected Distance: A small value (because these points are close despite the longitude difference)

6. **Invalid Input:**
   - Try entering non-numeric values or latitudes outside the range -90 to +90. The program should handle this gracefully (ideally with error messages and prompting for re-entry or termination).
